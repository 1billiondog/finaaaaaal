Black Iris

The black iris is a striking flower with deep, velvety petals that appear almost black. Symbolizing mystery and elegance, it stands out in any garden. Thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight, this unique bloom captivates with its rare beauty.

Japanese Iris

The Japanese iris (Iris ensata) features large, vibrant blooms in white, pink, blue, and purple. Thriving in wet conditions, it's perfect for water gardens and ponds. Blooming in late spring to early summer, it symbolizes beauty and grace, adding elegance to any landscape.

Siberian Iris

The Siberian iris, with its slender foliage and vibrant blooms in shades of blue, purple, white, and yellow, brings elegance and charm to any garden setting. Hardy and adaptable, it thrives in various conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.

Beardless Iris

The beardless iris, including varieties like Siberian and Japanese irises, features elegant, beard-free blooms in vibrant colors such as blue, purple, yellow, and white. Blooming in late spring to early summer, these irises thrive in various conditions and add graceful beauty to gardens and water edges.

Iris speculatrix

"Iris speculatrix," known as the Rainbow iris, enchants with its multicolored petals, illuminating gardens with a mesmerizing array of hues. Thriving in partial sunlight and blooming in late spring, it adds a captivating touch of beauty to outdoor spaces.

Iris cedreti

Iris cedreti, discovered in Lebanon's cedar forests, features delicate purple and blue petals, symbolizing resilience in harsh environments; blooming in late spring, it adds a rare touch of beauty to its rugged habitat, standing as a testament to nature's enduring grace.
